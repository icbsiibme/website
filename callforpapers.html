Call for Papers

ICBSII 2022 is an opportunity for medical engineering professionals from around the world who are involved in the field of healthcare to submit their original research papers which have not been submitted elsewhere in other conference / journals. All registered and presented papers in the conference will be forwarded for inclusion in IEEE Digital Xplore (Approval pending).

The extended version of the papers will be published in the peer-reviewed journals based on session chairs recommendation. Best papers will be awarded under each track.

  • Bio - signals Track

    • Biomedical Signal Processing.
    • Cardiovascular & Respiratory Systems Engineering.
    • Cognitive Neuroscience.
    • Brain-Computer Interface.
    • Speech Signal Processing.
    • Machine learning algorithms for Signal analysis.

  • Medical Images Track

    • Biomedical Imaging and Data Visualization.
    • Computer-Aided and Automated Diagnosis.
    • Medical Image Mining & Retrieval system.
    • Optimization techniques.
    • Virtual Reality.

  • Medical Data analytics Track

    • Biomedical Signal Processing.
    • Development of tools for high dimensional medical data. 
    • Data Collection and Data Mining in e-Health Applications. 
    • E-Health Software Design. 
    • In-memory computation in Healthcare. 
    • Big data analytics for health data management

  • Medical Instrumentation Track

    • 3D Bio-printing.
    • Device Technologies & Biomedical Robotics.
    • Drug Delivery & Diagnostic Systems.
    • Internet of Things (IoT) and Fog Computing in Healthcare
    • Mobile Health and Wearable Sensor Networks.
    • Advances in Biomedical Instrumentation. 
    • Point of Care Diagnosis. 
    • Body area network.
    • Non-contact Physiological measurement system. 
    • Wearable, outdoor and home-based applications. 
    • Printable Electronics for Healthcare Applications
    • Healthcare telemetry and telemedicine. 
    • Remote diagnosis and patient management. 
    • 5 G for healthcare application. 
    • All-pervasive wireless systems for health applications
